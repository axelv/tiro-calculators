# IMWG Multiple Myeloma Response Criteria (2016)

> Standardized assessment of treatment response in multiple myeloma per the
> International Myeloma Working Group (IMWG) 2016 consensus update.

---

## 1. Purpose

The IMWG response criteria provide a uniform, reproducible framework for
classifying treatment response in patients with multiple myeloma. They are
used in:

- Clinical trials (primary/secondary endpoint definition).
- Routine clinical practice (treatment-decision support, prognosis, switch
  triggers).
- Registry and outcomes reporting.

The 2016 update extended the historical criteria (sCR / CR / VGPR / PR / SD /
PD) by adding **minimal residual disease (MRD)-negative** categories that
quantify response depths below conventional CR using next-generation flow
cytometry (NGF), next-generation sequencing (NGS), and imaging
(PET-CT) modalities.

---

## 2. Inputs

All assessments compare a **current** measurement to the corresponding
**baseline** value (and, for progression, to the **best response (nadir)**
value). The required inputs are listed below.

### 2.1 Serum & urine paraprotein

| Input | Unit | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Serum M-protein (baseline) | g/dL | Quantitative serum protein electrophoresis (SPEP) at diagnosis or pre-treatment. |
| Serum M-protein (current) | g/dL | Quantitative SPEP at the assessment timepoint. |
| Urine M-protein (baseline) | mg / 24 h | 24-hour urine protein electrophoresis (UPEP). |
| Urine M-protein (current) | mg / 24 h | 24-hour UPEP at the assessment timepoint. |
| Serum immunofixation (IFE) | positive / negative | Required for CR / sCR. |
| Urine immunofixation (IFE) | positive / negative | Required for CR / sCR. |

### 2.2 Serum free light chains (FLC)

Used for patients with **non-measurable serum & urine M-protein** (oligo- or
non-secretory disease) when the involved/uninvolved FLC difference is
≥10 mg/dL (100 mg/L) at baseline.

| Input | Unit | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Involved FLC (κ or λ) | mg/L | At baseline and current timepoint. |
| Uninvolved FLC | mg/L | At baseline and current timepoint. |
| FLC difference (dFLC) | mg/L | dFLC = involved − uninvolved. |
| Serum κ/λ ratio | ratio | Normal range 0.26–1.65. |

### 2.3 Bone marrow

| Input | Unit | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bone marrow plasma cell % (baseline) | % | Required if baseline ≥30% or for non-secretory disease. |
| Bone marrow plasma cell % (current) | % | Aspirate or biopsy, whichever is higher. |
| Clonality assessment | clonal / polyclonal | By immunohistochemistry, immunofluorescence, or 2-/4-color flow. Required for sCR. |

### 2.4 Soft-tissue plasmacytomas

| Input | Unit | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Plasmacytoma presence at baseline | yes / no | By clinical exam or imaging. |
| Plasmacytoma SPD (baseline) | cm² | Sum of products of maximal perpendicular diameters of all measurable lesions. |
| Plasmacytoma SPD (current) | cm² | Same methodology as baseline. |
| New / enlarged lesions | yes / no | Definite increase in size for PD assessment. |

### 2.5 MRD assessment (for MRD-negative categories)

| Input | Unit / Method | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| NGF (next-generation flow) | EuroFlow-NGF, sensitivity ≥10⁻⁵ | Bone marrow aspirate, ≥2×10⁶ cells assessed. |
| NGS (next-generation sequencing) | LymphoSIGHT or validated equivalent, sensitivity ≥10⁻⁵ | DNA from bone marrow aspirate, baseline clone identified. |
| Imaging (PET-CT) | FDG-PET/CT | Resolution of any baseline tracer-avid lesion or decrease to mediastinal blood-pool SUV; or to lower than surrounding normal tissue. |
| Time of MRD assessment | months | Repeat ≥1 year apart for **sustained** MRD-negative. |

### 2.6 Hypercalcemia (for PD/clinical relapse)

| Input | Unit | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corrected serum calcium | mg/dL | Threshold >11.5 mg/dL solely attributable to plasma cell disorder. |

### 2.7 Baseline measurability

A patient is considered **measurable** when at least one of the following is
present at baseline (required for response evaluation):

- Serum M-protein ≥1 g/dL (≥10 g/L), or
- Urine M-protein ≥200 mg / 24 h, or
- Involved serum FLC ≥10 mg/dL (100 mg/L) **and** abnormal κ/λ ratio (when
  serum and urine M-protein are non-measurable).

## 3. Calculation — Categorical decision tree

Response category is assigned by evaluating the criteria in **order from the
deepest response (sustained MRD-negative) to the shallowest (PD)**. The first
category whose criteria are met is the assigned response. **Two consecutive
assessments** are required to confirm any response category; no intervening
therapy or progression may have occurred.

### 3.1 MRD-negative categories (require CR as a prerequisite)

| Category | Criteria |
| --- | --- |
| **Sustained MRD-negative** | MRD-negative in marrow (NGF or NGS, sensitivity 10⁻⁵) **and** by imaging if applicable, confirmed minimum **1 year apart**. Subsequent evaluations can be used to further specify duration (e.g., 5-year MRD-negative). |
| **Flow MRD-negative** | Absence of phenotypically aberrant clonal plasma cells by **NGF** on bone marrow aspirate using the EuroFlow standard operation procedure (or validated equivalent), with a minimum sensitivity of **1 in 10⁵ nucleated cells (10⁻⁵)**. |
| **Sequencing MRD-negative** | Absence of clonal plasma cells by **NGS** on bone marrow aspirate, defined as fewer than 2 identical sequencing reads obtained after DNA sequencing of bone marrow aspirate using the LymphoSIGHT platform (or validated equivalent), with a minimum sensitivity of **1 in 10⁵ nucleated cells (10⁻⁵)**. |
| **Imaging plus MRD-negative** | MRD-negative as defined by NGF or NGS **plus** disappearance of every area of increased tracer uptake found at baseline or a preceding PET-CT, **or** decrease to less than mediastinal blood-pool SUV, **or** decrease to less than that of surrounding normal tissue. |

### 3.2 Standard IMWG response categories

| Category | Criteria (all must be met) |
| --- | --- |
| **Stringent Complete Response (sCR)** | All criteria for **CR**, plus: <br>• Normal serum FLC ratio (**0.26–1.65**), **and** <br>• Absence of clonal plasma cells in bone marrow by immunohistochemistry, immunofluorescence, or 2-/4-color flow cytometry. |
| **Complete Response (CR)** | • Negative immunofixation of **serum and urine**, **and** <br>• Disappearance of any soft-tissue plasmacytomas, **and** <br>• **<5%** plasma cells in bone marrow. <br>For patients evaluable only by FLC: normal FLC ratio (0.26–1.65) in addition to the above (FLC-CR). |
| **Very Good Partial Response (VGPR)** | • Serum and urine M-protein detectable by immunofixation but **not on electrophoresis**, **or** <br>• **≥90%** reduction in serum M-protein **plus** urine M-protein **<100 mg / 24 h**. <br>For patients evaluable only by FLC: **≥90% reduction in dFLC**. |
| **Partial Response (PR)** | • **≥50%** reduction of serum M-protein, **and** <br>• Reduction in 24-h urinary M-protein by **≥90% or to <200 mg / 24 h**. <br>If serum and urine M-protein non-measurable: **≥50% decrease in dFLC** (involved minus uninvolved FLC). <br>If FLC also non-measurable: **≥50% reduction in bone marrow plasma cells** (provided baseline ≥30%). <br>If soft-tissue plasmacytomas present at baseline: **≥50% reduction** in the sum of products of perpendicular diameters (SPD). |
| **Minor Response (MR)** *(relapsed/refractory only)* | • **≥25% but ≤49%** reduction in serum M-protein, **and** <br>• Reduction in 24-h urine M-protein by **50–89%**. <br>If soft-tissue plasmacytomas present at baseline: **≥50%** reduction in SPD also required. <br>No increase in size or number of lytic bone lesions (compression fracture excluded). |
| **Stable Disease (SD)** | Not meeting criteria for CR, VGPR, PR, MR, or PD. *(Not recommended as a response indicator; stability characterized by time-to-progression estimates.)* |
| **Progressive Disease (PD)** | Any one of the following (confirmed on **2 consecutive assessments before next therapy**): <br>• **≥25% increase from lowest response value (nadir)** in serum M-protein, with absolute increase **≥0.5 g/dL** (or **≥1 g/dL** if nadir M-protein ≥5 g/dL); <br>• ≥25% increase from nadir in urine M-protein, absolute increase **≥200 mg / 24 h**; <br>• ≥25% increase from nadir in dFLC (only if serum/urine M-protein non-measurable), absolute increase **>10 mg/dL (100 mg/L)**; <br>• ≥25% increase from nadir in bone marrow plasma cell %, absolute increase **≥10%** (only if serum/urine M-protein and FLC non-measurable); <br>• Definite development of **new** soft-tissue plasmacytomas/bone lesions, or definite increase in size of existing lesions (≥50% **and** ≥1 cm in SPD); <br>• Development of **hypercalcemia** (corrected serum calcium **>11.5 mg/dL**) attributable solely to plasma cell proliferative disorder. |
| **Clinical Relapse** | One or more direct indicators of increasing disease and/or end-organ dysfunction (CRAB features) attributed to disease: new soft-tissue plasmacytomas or bone lesions, definite increase in size of existing plasmacytomas/bone lesions (≥50% and ≥1 cm), hypercalcemia (>11.5 mg/dL), hemoglobin decrease ≥2 g/dL not related to therapy or other non-myeloma conditions, rise in serum creatinine ≥2 mg/dL from start of therapy and attributable to myeloma, hyperviscosity related to serum paraprotein. |
| **Relapse from CR** *(used when endpoint is disease-free survival)* | Reappearance of serum or urine M-protein by immunofixation or electrophoresis; ≥5% plasma cells in bone marrow; or appearance of any other sign of progression (new plasmacytoma, lytic lesion, hypercalcemia). |
| **Relapse from MRD-negative** *(used when endpoint is MRD-negative DFS)* | Loss of MRD-negative status (positive NGF/NGS or imaging reappearance of disease); reappearance of serum/urine M-protein by IFE or electrophoresis; ≥5% clonal plasma cells in bone marrow; appearance of any other sign of progression. |

### 3.3 Decision-tree algorithm

```
1. Patient evaluable for response?
   - Confirm baseline measurability (M-protein / urine / FLC / marrow).

2. Assess deepest category first:
   a. If CR criteria met -> evaluate MRD:
        i.   Imaging-MRD + NGF/NGS negative & PET-CT clear -> Imaging+MRD-negative
        ii.  NGF negative (10^-5)                          -> Flow MRD-negative
        iii. NGS negative (10^-5)                          -> Sequencing MRD-negative
        iv.  Both i/ii/iii confirmed >=1 yr apart          -> Sustained MRD-negative
        v.   sCR criteria met (FLC ratio normal + no clonal PCs) -> sCR
        vi.  Otherwise                                      -> CR

   b. Else if VGPR criteria met -> VGPR
   c. Else if PR  criteria met -> PR
   d. Else if MR  criteria met (relapsed/refractory only) -> MR
   e. Else if PD  criteria met -> PD (or Clinical Relapse / Relapse from CR
                                        / Relapse from MRD-negative as applicable)
   f. Else                                                  -> SD

3. Confirm response on 2 consecutive assessments before next therapy.
```

### 3.4 Rules & caveats

- **Non-secretory / oligosecretory disease**: use the FLC criteria; if FLC also
  non-measurable, fall back to bone marrow plasma cell % (baseline ≥30%
  required for PR).
- **Confirmation**: all response categories require **two consecutive
  assessments** before a new therapy is started; radiographic studies are
  not required to confirm response unless used for primary measurement.
- **MRD-negative requires CR**: a patient cannot be classified as MRD-negative
  without first meeting CR criteria.
- **Sustained MRD-negative**: requires confirmation **≥1 year apart** with no
  intervening MRD-positive results. Longer durations (e.g., 5-yr) may be
  reported as sub-classifications.
- **Plasmacytomas**: plasmacytoma reduction is measured by sum of products of
  perpendicular diameters (SPD) of all measurable lesions.
- **Progression "from nadir"**: percentages and absolute increases are
  always relative to the lowest (best) value previously achieved.

## 4. Output

A single categorical assignment per assessment, drawn from the ordered set:

```
Sustained MRD-negative
   > Imaging plus MRD-negative
      > Flow MRD-negative
         > Sequencing MRD-negative
            > Stringent Complete Response (sCR)
               > Complete Response (CR)
                  > Very Good Partial Response (VGPR)
                     > Partial Response (PR)
                        > Minor Response (MR)              [relapsed/refractory only]
                           > Stable Disease (SD)
                              > Progressive Disease (PD) / Clinical Relapse
                                 / Relapse from CR / Relapse from MRD-negative
```

```json
}
```
